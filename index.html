INTRO TO THE BLOG

Bringing Online Learning to schools and underserved communities with OPPIA

OPPIA is an educational technology platform that seeks to provide quality education to students in underserved communities and schools. The platform offers online lessons that are both interactive and engaging, featuring captivating stories and a virtual tutor.

Its goal is to enable learners worldwide to access high-quality education that is enjoyable and interactive. Additionally, OPPIA's lessons are being translated into various languages to ensure that students can understand the content without having to learn a new language.

Moreover, this platform is entirely free of charge.

This blog post recounts my experience carrying out a user research and go-to-market exercise for the Oppia lessons web and mobile application in government primary schools. In carry out this task, my user research and go-to-market plan involved:

Identify the Questions I want answers to at the end of the research:

"What are the students' initial attitudes towards the educational web app?:"

The were excited and eager to use the application

"How do the primary school students interact with the educational web app?"

Most of the students found the app easy to use, they learn how to use and navigate the app quickly

"What challenges or difficulties do the primary school students encounter while using the educational web app"

Some students found unconventional names like Nic and Jamie confusing.

"What features of the educational web app are most useful to the primary school students?"

The story based feature was very useful, one of the student was able to explain a question in the multiplication based on the story to her partner.

As Oppia's educational materials cater to learners aged 7 to 14, who are usually enrolled in primary levels 4 and 5 and 6. I opted to work with ten male and ten female students from each class.

Develop the Pilot Plan

I outlined the methods and procedures that will be used to collect and analyze data. The procedures included the following:

  1. Diagnostic test: the diagnostic test has 2 questions from each math section (Place value, Addition and subtraction, Multiplication, Division, Fraction, Ratios & Proportions, and percentages.) The survey helped figure out which topic the student should be learning.
  2. Pre-test: Administer a pre-test survey to the primary school students to test the studentsâ€™ knowledge on certain subjects before taking Oppia lessons on the same subject.
  3. Taking lessons and observation: I observed the students using the Oppia app in the classroom,I ensured that users were able to use the app correctly, I assisted them when they needed some help with the use of the app, I also gathered information about how they interact with the app and identified the challenges or difficulties they encounter.
  4. Post-test: I Administered a post-test survey to the students to gather feedback on their experience using the educational web app and to identify any improvements or modifications that could be made to the app.

THE JOURNEY OF INTRODUCING OPPIA TO SCHOOLS

On the 17th of March 2023, I paid a visit to Olumo Primary School where I had the opportunity to meet with Mr. Solomon, the assistant Headmaster. During our meeting, I introduced the Oppia lessons app to him, elaborated on the goals and mission of the Oppia foundation, and presented our proposal seeking his consent to run a pilot with some students.

However, Mr. Solomon informed me that I needed the approval of the Education secretary of the local government to carry out the pilot in the school. Subsequently, I proceeded to meet with the Education secretary where I submitted my proposal and it was approved.

LEARNING IS FUN AND INTERACTIVE WITH OPPIA

At 11:30am, the pilot commenced with students from primary 4, 5, and 6. The session was divided into three parts, one for each grade level. During the pilot, two mobile phones with the android app and one laptop with the web application were available for use. The devices were shared among the students, with two students sharing one device. Although they learned together with the devices, each student answered the questions independently.

I introduced the app to the students and explained how the session would run, and they were excited to begin.

The intervention and impact of Oppia can continue on an ongoing basis

The Oppia lesson app received a warm reception from various stakeholders, including the Education Secretary, the School's Headmaster, the teachers, and the students.

Education Secretary expressed appreciation for the fact that the app is free and easily accessible, describing it as a welcomed development.

The Assistant Headmaster also praised the initiative, stating that it would be highly appreciated by teachers, students, and parents.
